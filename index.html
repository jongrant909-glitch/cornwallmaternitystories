Hi there, Here’s my story….. My baby (firstborn) was born in the summer of 2023 at Treliske hospital. It was a rollercoaster of emotions met with both warm, genuine care and ice cold, dismissive attitudes. My waters broke and 12 hours later still no contractions. I was sent up to the hospital to be induced, something I wasn’t keen for. I recall a wonderful midwife going through all my options but my anxiety was through the roof already. She agreed to give me some time to think through my options with my partner and we chose to return home for a few hours, try and relax, have some good food and see if we could bring about contractions naturally. Nothing worked so we returned to treliske and I was seen by a junior doctor. She was nice, but pretty firm that my birth plan was out the window at this point, and I had to be admitted to the delivery ward and be induced. I very reluctantly agreed to a pessary in the first instance and be admitted, but remained clear I was not ready to be induced by oxytocin. As time went on, both me and baby were being monitored and we were both fine. I wanted to carry on waiting for contractions to start naturally but the pressure from (some) of the midwives and in particular the medical staff started mounting. They moved from F1 (junior docs) up through the ranks trying to get me to agree to have an oxytocin drip. When I still declined they brought in the consultant. A stern woman who spoke to me in such a belittling manner I almost burst into tears. Once it became clear to her she was doing nothing but infuriating me (raising both my stress levels and heart rate) she turned to my partner, back to me and told him directly that if I did not comply I was putting both myself and baby at high risk of death. I watched the colour drain out of his face, his eyes grow wide with fear and look at me pleading with me to agree. I have never in my life felt so vulnerable, and for a supposed healthcare provider to undermine me in the midst of that vulnerability, without any compassion or support was beyond belief. I could not believe what had just happened. Both baby and me were still being monitored and still no signs of distress. I knew I was approaching their policy of how long to feasibly wait before inducing, something which I believe had been reduced by 24 hours. I was told the pessary hadn’t worked and I need to start oxytocin. I felt exhausted, deflated, anxious, sad, scared, angry. We’d been in hospital for about 24 hours at this point. I very reluctantly agreed. I remember sobbing when the midwife started the drip. Time went on. Contractions started. I felt desperately out of control. I was never once asked if I was ok? Did I want to talk about it? Could anything be done to help? My partner and I were left a room for hours, only to be checked on when the baby monitor had moved / was beeping or the drip needed increasing. I then reached that point during labour when your body says push. I told my partner I needed to push. He told the midwife I needed to push. Her words, “no you don’t, you were only 3cm on your last check”. I thought, oh maybe I don’t. We waited it out, but sure enough that feeling returned. “Babe I need to push, please get the midwife again”. She returns, disagrees with me I need to push but checks me anyway, only to find that I am in fact at 10cm and yes, I need to push. Baby was born without issue, taking only 20 minutes to make his way into the world. Generally my care from then on was great apart from one final, negative experience. Baby and I were moved from the delivery ward, arriving at around midnight. My partner was told to leave (standard for them, terrifying for me!). We made it through our first night, with me and baby sleeping through. I was woken in the morning to find a somewhat disgruntled midwife asking me how many times I fed my baby overnight as I hadn’t filled out the chart. I hadn’t fed him at all, he slept for most of the night and when he did stir, I couldn’t get him to latch. She berated me for not alerting anyone. She made me feel completely inadequate. She brought me to tears. Once again, in my most vulnerable state I had been brought to my knees by someone who was supposed to care. I had, on the advice of a close friend, brought with me some colostrum so we gave my baby that however I was convinced I was a terrible mother at this point. Then the nurse I’d encountered at the very beginning of my journey came on shift. The one who was kind, warm and caring. She came over to say congratulations, she asked me how I was and I just broke down. The first person to genuinely care, or so it felt. I told her about the breastfeeding issue to which she said I’d been given Pethidine shortly before he was born. The medication was undoubtedly in his system which is why he slept all night and struggled to latch. A simple explanation which had it been given earlier, most certainly would have avoided a lot of heartache. She sat with me and together we got him to latch. I wish I could remember her name, she was so wonderful. So that’s my story. I’d like to know more about what you’re going to be doing with this information? Kind regards, Charlotte
